Steps to make Vegetable Salad

  • The base of a green salad is crunchy leaves that can stand on their own or stay firm when dressed
  • Crisp lettuces are easy to eat torn, or cut into bite-sized pieces across the stem. For greens with a fibrous stem such as kale
  • A variety of textures is key to a good salad. Add a roughly equal amount of arugula, spinach, butter leaf lettuce, or another soft
  • Handle these gently while washing and chopping, as soft leaves tend to bruise more easily.
  • Serve cold tomato salad for a summer appetizer.
  • Include a few vegetables that don't normally go into a raw salad
  • Serve with French salad dressing, Vinaigrette, or just shake together balsamic vinegar and olive oil.
